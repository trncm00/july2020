I have been taking a math course called Computational Thinking on Coursera. I am in week six. It has been a challenge, but I never saw myself making magic squares or learning mathematical induction before.

Hey there July I.

I remember when I first got into computers, it was when my father had his apple backed up, back in what must have been 89 or thereabouts. He had this dude with a beard and earth shoes come by. I don't know where he got him, he hustled him from off the LIRR or something and got him to back up this 50 pound computer he kept his professional work on. I think it might have been because I magnetized the thing, or that was an earlier incident, not related. Any way, I come in and the guy had the computer backing up on tape reel to reels. Or that is how I remember it, this was well before you could back up a computer by pressing a button, I just remember the guy hunched over the keyboard typing in commands. I had never seen it's like, and I was shooed away before long but what I had witnessed stuck with me for the rest of my life.

Hey there July II.

I grew up in an area of Central Islip. It was a nice area mostly, diverse for Long Island, close to a large national park and forty-five minutes to Queens by car. It was a lucky thing, but the town had it's foibles. Central Islip was weak to addiction and when you went up around the Pilgrim's you had to be on your guard. When we were young, my friends and I wouldn't ride up past Lowell avenue, though we would run far afield, for miles and miles, in every other direction. Now there is a condominium and a nature path and a big courthouse, a huge development basically. But the past, the bloody past of the Pilgrims Mental Institution came back a few years ago, at least in my mind, when a group of young men were hacked and beaten to death in a MS13-style attack. I am not merely parroting the words of Trump or that malevolent little lawn jockey, whats-his-noodle Jefferson Beauragard Sessions, jesus, let me tell you, when I saw him in CI I perked the hell up. No, I did some small research, they got some teenager to inform on them and when he got deported, they sent him back with the criminal gang he had ratted out. ICE hit my hometown pretty hard.
